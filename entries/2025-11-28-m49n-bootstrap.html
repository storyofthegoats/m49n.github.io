interpreted ai
Tonight I finally gave shape to an idea that’s been floating around for a while: a place where I can think out loud, privately, but still have enough structure that it feels like a system instead of a mess. I bought m49n.com, wrestled with GitHub Pages, broke things, reset things, and slowly rebuilt a clean landing page. It was equal parts nostalgic and frustrating — echoes of teaching myself HTML years ago, except now I care about typography, structure, and mood instead of animated GIFs. The important realization was that I don’t want a blog, and I don’t want to feed a content machine. I want a ledger: a curated surface where only the entries that matter make it through. The rest can stay in private journals, notes, and throwaway drafts. The dual-view idea feels like the right compromise between honesty and clarity. On one side: my raw words, unedited. On the other: a cleaned, organized, sometimes more brutally honest interpretation. If I keep doing this, future me will be able to see both what I said and what I meant. Somewhere between those two panels is the version of me I’m actually trying to understand.
noted themes / actions
- keep the site small and intentional; no obligation to post constantly. - use this dual-view format for selected entries only. - let the real journaling happen elsewhere, then graduate pieces here. - treat m49n as a ledger, not a performance.
